The Pioneer in Latest Spine Surgeries in India

Minimally invasive spine surgery (MISS) is an endoscopic procedure that uses specialized video cameras and instruments that are passed through small incisions in the back, chest or abdomen to access the spine and perform the surgery. Apollo Hospitals offers minimally invasive spinal surgery, which generally entails less pain and blood loss, a shorter hospital stay, and outcomes when compared to open surgery. For most patients, minimally invasive spinal surgery can resolve disabling problems and facilitate a return to normal activities.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

1. Whats the length of stay for minimally invasive spine surgery?

With minimally invasive spine surgery, patients have a less length of stay in the hospital. The exact length varies with each individual patient and procedure

2. How long does it take to recover from minimally invasive back surgery?

You should usually be able to return to routine activities in about six weeks

3. What are the advantages of minimally invasive spine surgery?

When compared to open surgery, minimally invasive spine surgery includes better cosmetic results from smaller incisions, less blood loss, pain and infection
